# Code of conduct

BGOBS welcomes people of every background and experience level. Project spaces
must remain useful, safe, and focused on improving the software.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful and patient, especially when explaining unfamiliar tools.
- Critique ideas and changes, not people.
- Give actionable feedback and acknowledge uncertainty.
- Respect privacy, consent, attribution, and project licenses.
- Help de-escalate conflict and accept reasonable maintainer decisions.

## Unacceptable behavior

Harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized attention, deliberate
misgendering, doxxing, publishing private communications, sustained disruption,
and other conduct that makes participation unsafe are not accepted. This also
applies when someone represents the project in another public space.

## Reporting and enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to `d.github@chey.net`. Do not use a public
issue when doing so would expose a target or confidential details. The
maintainer may edit or remove content, issue a warning, temporarily restrict
participation, or permanently exclude a participant, proportionate to the
behavior and risk.

Reports will be handled as confidentially as practical. Retaliation against a
person who raises a good-faith concern is itself a violation. If the maintainer
is involved in a report, use GitHub's abuse-reporting tools so the complaint is
not routed only to the person concerned.
